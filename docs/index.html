How it works

1

A gentle chime plays

At regular intervals, a soft tone plays and your screen edges glow. A mindfulness quote appears. Time to check in with yourself.

2

Slide to the edge

Move your mouse to the left edge for "present" or the right edge for "not present." Hold for two seconds. Feel the haptic feedback as it fills.

3

Your rhythm adapts

Stay present twice in a row and the interval grows. Drift away and it shortens. Over time, Zen finds your natural attention span.

Designed to disappear

Invisible by design

No windows. No popups. Just screen edges and haptic feedback. Zen stays out of your way until you need it.

Body awareness

Every few minutes, a gentle reminder: unclench your jaw, soften your shoulders, feel your feet on the ground.

Wisdom tradition

100 Buddha quotes on presence, letting go, and the nature of mind. A new one with each check-in.

"Do not dwell in the past, do not dream of the future, concentrate the mind on the present moment."

Buddha

Start being present

Zen starts at 45 seconds. As your practice deepens, the intervals grow. There is no goal. Only awareness.
